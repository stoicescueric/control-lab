## 1. Introduction

Every preceding lesson contributed one component of an autonomous routine: discretisation and units
(the execution substrate), PID and feedforward (the actuator-level controllers), odometry (state
estimation), and pure pursuit (path following). A competitive routine is the *composition* of all of
them under a time budget. This paper studies that composition in simulation and on hardware.

## 2. Method

The simulation advances a kinematic robot model at a fixed timestep, driving it along generated
trajectories with the same controllers deployed on the robot. Each subsystem links back to its
lesson, so the method is auditable end-to-end:

- **State estimation** — constant-curvature odometry from dead-wheel pods ([Odometry & Pose](../localization-odometry/odometry.mdx)).
- **Reference generation** — trapezoidal/S-curve motion profiles bounding velocity and acceleration.
- **Path following** — pure-pursuit curvature with velocity feedforward ([Path Following](../path-following/pure-pursuit.mdx), [Feedforward](../control-theory/feedforward.mdx)).
