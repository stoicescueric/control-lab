# Module 6 - Advanced Research

This module is the capstone: the point where the curriculum stops being only FTC control theory and becomes an original engineering case study. The research problem is not "how do we shoot a ball?" It is more precise:

> How can a robot compensate a projectile shot in real time while the projectile loses energy to drag, the launcher loses energy to contact, and the chassis is still moving?

The answer is a pipeline:

1. Build a physical simulator that is honest about drag and numerical integration.
2. Calibrate the real launcher instead of trusting ideal energy transfer.
3. Control the flywheel so it recovers after each shot.
4. Read sparse calibration tables without inventing unsafe overshoot.
5. Aim at a virtual target when the robot is moving.

## Lessons

- [**Simulating Physical Reality**](./trajectory-simulation.mdx) - RK4 vs. Euler, vacuum vs. drag, and wheel-speed-to-exit-velocity transfer losses.
- [**On-Bot Control & Dynamic Targeting**](./shoot-on-the-move.mdx) - flywheel recovery, monotone interpolation, and fixed-point shoot-on-the-move aiming.
