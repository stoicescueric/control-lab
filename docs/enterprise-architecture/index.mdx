# Module 1 · Enterprise Architecture & Loop Optimization

Every controller in this curriculum runs once per loop iteration, and every loop iteration costs time.
Before the math matters, the *infrastructure* around it has to be sound: a fast, measured loop; live
tuning over Wi-Fi; and a version-control workflow that lets a team iterate without breaking the robot
the night before a competition.

## What this module covers

- **Git workflow for robotics teams** — branching, review, and keeping a deployable `main`.
- **FTC Dashboard** — live telemetry and tuning controller gains over Wi-Fi without re-deploying.
- **Manual bulk reads** — collapsing dozens of per-call I²C round-trips into one, the single biggest
  loop-time win available to most teams.
- **Loop-time optimization** — measuring `Δt`, finding the bottleneck, and protecting the budget your
  controllers depend on.

:::warning Loop time is a hardware reality, not a detail
A PID derivative term and a discrete integral both divide or multiply by `Δt`. If your loop time is
inconsistent — say it jumps from 5 ms to 40 ms because of an unbatched sensor read — your controller's
behavior changes with it. This module is the foundation that makes discretization — and every
controller downstream — trustworthy.
:::
