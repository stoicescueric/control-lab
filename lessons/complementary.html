The Complementary Filter

Two flawed sensors, fused into one good answer — the trick that keeps drones level.

Sometimes one sensor isn't enough, but you have two sensors that fail in opposite ways. A complementary filter blends them so each one covers the other's weakness. The classic example lives inside every drone, phone, and Segway: figuring out which way is "up" (the tilt angle).

Meet the two imperfect sensors

📐

Accelerometer

Feels gravity, so it knows the true tilt on average — but it's noisy and gets fooled by every bump and vibration. Good long-term, jittery short-term.

🌀

Gyroscope

Measures how fast you're rotating. Buttery smooth and instant — but you must add up its readings to get an angle, and tiny errors pile up into drift. Great short-term, useless long-term.

🧩
The insight: the gyro is great at fast/short-term, the accelerometer at slow/long-term. So pass the gyro through a high-pass (keep fast), pass the accelerometer through a low-pass (keep slow), and add them. They complement each other — hence the name.

The formula (it's basically the low-pass again)

angle = α × ( angle + gyro × dt ) + (1 − α) × accelAngle

The first part "coasts" the angle forward using the smooth gyro. The second part gently tugs it back toward the accelerometer's honest (if noisy) reading, killing the drift. α is usually very close to 1 (like 0.98) — mostly trust the gyro, sip a little accelerometer to stay honest.

Watch all three fail and succeed
