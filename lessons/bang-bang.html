On/Off (Bang-Bang) Control

The simplest controller there is — and it's heating your home right now.

Not every controller needs to be clever. Bang-bang control (also called on/off or hysteresis control) has exactly two settings: full ON and full OFF. Too cold? Heater fully on. Warm enough? Heater fully off. That's it. Your home thermostat, your fridge, a kettle, an electric oven — all bang-bang.

🚰 Analogy. It's like filling a glass by slapping the tap fully open, then fully shut, over and over — versus PID, which is like easing the tap to a precise trickle. Cruder, but dead simple and impossible to "mis-tune."

The one wrinkle: the deadband

If the heater flipped on and off the instant you crossed the target, it would chatter — switching thousands of times a second, wearing itself out. The fix is a deadband (or hysteresis): a little gap around the target where nothing changes.

  • Turn ON only when temperature drops below target − ½ band.
  • Turn OFF only when it rises above target + ½ band.
  • In between, leave it exactly as it is. That gap stops the frantic switching.

Play thermostat

Set the deadband width and watch the trade-off. A narrow band keeps the temperature very close to target but the heater switches constantly (clicky and wearing). A wide band switches rarely but lets the room swing further from comfy.

Bang-bang vs. PID

Bang-bangPID
OutputJust ON or OFFAny smooth amount
TuningOne knob (the deadband)Three gains, real skill
ResultAlways sawtooths a bitCan settle perfectly still
Best forHeaters, fridges, pumps — slow systems where a little wobble is fine Drones, motors, anything needing precision & smoothness
💡
Why it survives: bang-bang is unbeatable when your actuator can't do "half power" anyway (a relay, a valve, a compressor) and when the system is slow enough that a gentle sawtooth doesn't matter. Simple, robust, basically free. Don't over-engineer.

Bang-bang and PID both only react to the present. The final controller is the mastermind that simulates the future before it acts: Model Predictive Control.
